The Awards and assessment criteria

Hospital Pharmacist of the Year

The Hospital Pharmacist of the Year Award celebrates a pharmacist who has consistently demonstrated a commitment to innovation, excellence and success for bringing the right medicine to the right patient at the right time over the last twelve months.

Lifetime Achievement Award

The Lifetime Achievement Award acknowledges a pharmacist who has consistently gone above and beyond over the duration of their career to make a difference to the lives of patients, and advocate the pharmacy profession. Nominees can be retired or semi-retired but must still be active in the field. Nominations must have 25+ years’ service.

Rising Star Award

The Rising Star Award celebrates a pharmacist who has shown real ambition and innovation when it comes to improving patient outcomes and are starting to make a name for themselves. Candidates for this Award must be in the first 5 years of their career post-qualification as a pharmacist.

The Extra Mile Award

Celebrating those who have gone the extra mile. It is an award for an occasion where a pharmacist has thought outside the box and explored all options to achieve a better patient outcome.

Pharmacist Team of the Year

Open to all hospital pharmacist teams. Celebrating team spirit and collaboration. This is a recognition of the whole team, and successes that they have had and made possible.

As a general guideline, we would like to see examples of any achievements of the nominee relevant to the Award assessment criteria, information on their relationships with both patients and colleagues, and any long-term goals or vision for the future they may have.

How to nominate

Nominations must be made using the online entry form made available on the Clinigen Direct section of Clinigen Group plc’s website.

All mandatory fields must be completed.

It is the responsibility of the nominator to articulate how the nominee has met the assessment criteria set out above.

Where appropriate, you may provide examples to support your submission, however, please ensure no patient data is included in your response.

The deadline for receipt of nominations is midnight UK time on 23 February 2020.

Both self-nominations and nominations made by a colleague/associate are acceptable except that self-nominations may not be made for the Lifetime Achievement Award.

It is essential that both the nominator and nominees agree to these Terms and Conditions and that both nominator and nominees are aware that the submission has been made. By submitting a nomination using the online entry form, you agree on your own behalf and on behalf of any nominees that you and your nominees agree to be bound by these Term and Conditions.

Award process

Judging will consist of three rounds of reviews, shortlisting and selection. All winners will be subject to verification.

Reviews, shortlisting, and selection will be undertaken by a panel consisting of Clinigen employees (“Panel”).

As part of the process, the Panel may be in touch to request additional information or materials, such as a short biography or a high-quality photograph of nominees.

All decisions will be made by the Panel based on the submissions provided and their assessment relevant to the assessment criteria, to the exclusion of any other factors and in the Panel’s discretion.

The Event and the Awards do not constitute an inducement to recommend and/or prescribe, purchase, supply, sell or administer any medicine.

The Awards will comprise a certificate or similar. No gifts for personal benefit or cash or cash equivalent will be awarded.

All decisions of the Panel are final and no correspondence or discussion will be entered into.

Winners will be notified approximately four weeks in advance of the Event to confirm their availability to attend the Event to receive their Award. Clinigen is unable to pay any costs of attendance.
